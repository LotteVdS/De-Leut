August was wonderfully boring. I had no projects to finish and no longer the drive to start any anew. I could be reading Fantastic Four comics one moment. Then still have time left to download some old PC games and play for the nostalgia of it all.

When accidentally cheating on my husband with Mike Delfino, and tending to my garden in the virtual gameworld of Desperate Housewives, became boring too... I turned to my family. That's how I ended up here, legs hooked over the back of the couch and trying not to let my curls tickle my sister's shins, scrolling through my notes app. "I like Stella," I said. It felt ridiculous to even have to defend the choice. "It means from the stars, it sounds Italian and hello? Winx Club!" My sister scrunched her nose. I'm sure, sometimes, she wondered how we were even related.



Even by the time my dad came home, my sister and I were still debating. "Mine will be Atlas," she said and I would reply, "That might clash with my Astro though." She would go, "I claim Noah." while I would be typing out Delphine. She'd be strongly against my choice of Johnny until I caved and changed it to Jonathan. And she would almost agree with me on Odette. August was wonderfully boring. And so was picking apart fictional babynames with my family for days.

Every now and then, her phone buzzed for so long, it slipped to the edge of the kitchen table. Creeping closer out of sight and out of mind. Almost completely ignored and forgotten. But not even a beat later, the familiar weight would be back in her hands and her eyes would scour the new notifications, counting them as quickly as she dismissed them.

A frown settled on her face. No news. A sigh followed suit. Nothing of importance, anyways. You know how they say "don't shoot the messenger"? But how about... missing the messenger? How about getting ads and attention aplenty and still only holding onto hope that the one conversation she yearns for the most, might continue today? Good God, it was a cruel fate to wait.

Another buzz pulled her from her reverie. She glanced down, her fingers hesitating over the screen. Now this message, you could say, was one of fate's unexpected twists. Not who she was waiting for, not by a long shot. But... her eyes lingered on the preview of his message. It was bold, deserving an eye roll and sly smile. Maybe a little inappropriate. Definitely not the guy on her mind, no... but... maybe not entirely unwelcome either.

What do Hercules and the Cheshire cat have in common? Think.

A game of Codenames is taking up the table. I look at the Disney-themed cards on display, waiting for the one braincell to pop up and shout out a connection. Codename: olympian smile! But it stays quiet. My eyes linger around the table for a moment longer to enjoy. There's plates with crumbs left from all the desserts we've shared. The next boardgame is sitting ready to be tried out. And, even more so than usual, I'm grateful to be surrounded by some of the best friends.

Just last week, I'd logged back into Discord and caught up with online friends I hadn't heard from in forever. (It never felt like forever. It felt like coming home.) And now, I get to hang out peacefully, at my own pace, with another horde of ever-lovely girls? My luck these days. It knows no limits.

"Heroes in disguise," I say, making my move, at last. Thoughtful frowns settled on my current team mates. Soon, the teams would switch around again so everyone would get a chance to play alongside the birthday girl. Didn't keep any competitiveness at bay, though. My face contorted as I heard their whispering about possible answers - for them to guess, for me to know. Hold it in. Hold it together. That was the hardest part for me, after all... learning to keep a pokerface. Every one of my friends knows that. (And so does my family, my work family and probably anyone who's ever met me during a game of Uno or while trying to throw a surprise.)

2024 had its turbulent start. When in therapy, I would cry for feeling selfish and a bother to those I'd call friends. Every hangout, I would feel guilty for not enjoying myself or their presence as I was overrun with other worries. But only more love grew from those experiences, as every single one of my friends stuck around. Those who I met through my laptop, or through college, through boybands and other friends, even those through sheer luck. Those friends I share froyo with and those I try to upsell on ice cream at the beach (especially in winter. It's the best then!). Those I always meet in big groups at fun outings and those I usually just lounge around with in private, enjoying strawberries in the park or a classic trilogy from their couch. Those who I text five compliments per minute and those who accidentally leave me on read for days and months on end (same, by the way). They're always there, for longing and belonging.

"I just want to say, I had the loveliest time today. You are all just so wonderful."

'Twas another dreadful day of chores. Protecting the house. Making sure everyone gets enough fresh air. And perhaps the most important of all, keeping everyone from getting fat. If I have to sacrifice myself to cut the cheese intake in this household in half, so be it.

Some days my job was easier than others. Depends on who's home, really. Joost goes out two to three times a day. Sometimes he doesn't even need me to accompany him and I can trust he always comes back safely. Britt is usually out all day helping other furry friends. I can smell how popular she's becoming at her veterinary job. Xander's taking care of his own household now, the first of a generation. I know he's good. But then there's Lotte...

"Woof!" Yes, it's 12PM and I have to bark at the stairs just for her to come down. I swear, it's like she's not listening. "Go out!", I tell her, "Meet some friends! Or at least, make them.". But she's already jumped on the couch and tapped in behind her laptop. "Woof woof!" At least ask some guy over to hug instead of that blanket you've been hiding out under for the last three days. I'm just saying... She's home alone, for crying out loud.

She gets up for only two things: going to the toilet and pudding. With a sigh, I get up and follow her suit. She's one of those 'gotta keep in sight at all times' types, you know? (Plus, I don't mind a sweet little treat every now and then for my service.) Back on the couch, I settle down closeby and watch with frowned, tired eyes. Now she's taking out the phone? While she's on the laptop? Good god, even barking will do no good. She's in her own little world.

"Thanks for guarding me," I smile brightly as I spot Fons on the hallway floor. Bending down to show he's earned himself a little pet, only earns me the deepest sigh in a dog's existence. Almost existantial. And for what?

"Look, I'm sorry you're not in De Leut this month. It's blog-themed! I just don't see how a dog fits in a blog! And it's not like you've done anything special this month." My yapping is soon interrupted by Fons' deep howl. Well deserved, surely. I swallow the rest of my apology and think. "Well, maybe I can fit you in... a fun little anecdote about how you love to lick my pudding lids, maybe? And otherwise there's always September."
